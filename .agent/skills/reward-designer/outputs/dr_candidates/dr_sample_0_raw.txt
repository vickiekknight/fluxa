```python
# Domain randomization for robot end-effector to target pose task.
# Goal: Move end-effector to target 3D pose smoothly, avoiding jerks.
# Success: End-effector within 5cm of target position with correct orientation.
# Training in simulation, deployment in real world.

# Reasoning for each parameter:

# joint_friction_range:
# Friction is a physical property that can vary significantly between simulations and the real world,
# and even between different real-world environments. Higher friction can make the robot
# sluggish and require more force to move. Randomizing friction can help the policy learn
# to be robust to varying levels of resistance.
joint_friction_range = [0.0, 3.0] # Choosing a range that covers a good portion of the spectrum, but not the extreme to avoid making learning too difficult.

# joint_armature_range:
# Armature represents the inertia of the robot's links and is often simplified in simulation.
# Real-world robots have complex inertia properties due to their physical construction and
# how they are mounted. Randomizing armature can help the policy generalize to different
# dynamic characteristics of the robot.
joint_armature_range = [0.0, 0.5] # A moderate range to introduce variations in inertia without drastically changing robot dynamics.

# joint_stiffness_scale_range:
# Joint stiffness (Kp) determines how strongly the actuator resists displacement from its
# desired position. In the real world, this can be affected by factors like motor wear,
# temperature, and calibration. Randomizing stiffness can help the policy learn to
# control the robot even when its joint stiffness is not perfectly tuned.
joint_stiffness_scale_range = [0.7, 1.3] # A tight range around the default value, as extreme stiffness can lead to instability or slow response.

# joint_damping_scale_range:
# Joint damping (Kd) is crucial for absorbing energy and preventing oscillations. It's
# another parameter that can vary in the real world due to factors like motor internal
# damping and control loop implementation. Randomizing damping can improve the policy's
# ability to achieve smooth motions and avoid jerky movements.
joint_damping_scale_range = [0.7, 1.3] # Similar to stiffness, a moderate range around the default value is appropriate to encourage smooth control without causing extreme over/under-damping.
```
